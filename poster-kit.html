Posters

Public copy for a food-resilience push.

The invitation should feel practical, not preachy: fuel and freight shocks make food security local again, and every safe garden, pantry, freezer and kitchen can help.

Families, Elders and neighbours

Grow It. Share It. Don't Waste It.

Got extra tomatoes, herbs, lemons, freezer space or a good recipe? Shared Table helps trusted groups turn local food into a community buffer.

Start small: map what you have, use what is urgent, preserve what can wait, and share only with consent.

Youth and visitors

Good Food. Less Waste. Fewer Panic Shops.

Cook with what is fresh today. Share what would otherwise sit in the back of the fridge. Help turn leftovers into freezer packs, brunch, soup, chutney or something people actually want to eat.

Photos, posts and public notices come after consent. The meal matters more than the content.

Growers and knowledge holders

Local Harvest, Real Respect.

The table can celebrate local food, growing skills, fishers, farmers and cultural knowledge only where permission, safety and proper acknowledgement are clear.

Unusual, foraged or culturally sensitive foods stay out of the public feed until the right checks are complete.
