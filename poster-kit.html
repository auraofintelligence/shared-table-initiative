Families, Elders and neighbours

Pull Up a Chair. The Table is Set.

Got extra tomatoes, herbs, lemons or a good story to share? The Shared Table is a proposed community gathering where surplus food, local skills and good company can meet safely.

Start small: offer food, help with prep, bring a recipe, or sit for a yarn. The first version is about trust, not pressure.

Youth and visitors

Good Food. Less Waste. Island Vibes.

Cook with what is fresh today. Share what would otherwise sit in the back of the fridge. Help turn leftovers into something people actually want to eat.

Photos, posts and public notices come after consent. The meal matters more than the content.

Growers and knowledge holders

Local Harvest, Real Respect.

The table can celebrate local food, growing skills and cultural knowledge only where permission, safety and proper acknowledgement are clear.

Unusual, foraged or culturally sensitive foods stay out of the public feed until the right checks are complete.
