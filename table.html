The first meal can be tiny.

A good shared table might be six neighbours making soup, a brunch table using ripe fruit, a youth crew filling freezer packs, a club kitchen cooking after a working bee, or a private support circle planning meals for the week. Timing can follow the people: breakfast, brunch, lunch, afternoon, evening, late pickup or split-shift.

Pick a role that fits your day.

Participation should be easy. You do not have to be a chef, organiser or tech person to be useful.

Food sharer

Offer produce or pantry items with honest notes about storage, date, source and any doubts.

Kitchen helper

Wash, chop, cook, clean, label leftovers or keep the bench moving calmly.

Care connector

Check who needs food, transport, company or a quiet way to ask for help.

Driver

Move produce, containers or meals between trusted group members.

AI prompt buddy

Help someone use Kitchen Mate prompts without making them feel silly.
