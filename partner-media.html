Partner and media

Talk like a proposal until the proposal becomes real.

The source brief includes a sponsorship letter and media release. This page reframes them as careful draft materials, not launch claims.

Draft partner note

Foundational support for a small, reviewable trial.

The Straddie Shared Table is proposed as a community-led food sharing and surplus rescue trial for Minjerribah / North Stradbroke Island. The first practical goal is modest: test whether local surplus, volunteer time, safe preparation and public notices can be coordinated in a way that builds trust and reduces waste.

Potential partners would not be asked to endorse a complete system on day one. They would be invited to help shape a safe trial scope, review the evidence needs, clarify cultural and operational boundaries, and decide whether future funding pathways are appropriate.

Draft media angle

Community food, public notices and grant readiness.

Keep the media frame human and grounded: local surplus, safe sharing, practical civic infrastructure and careful collaboration. Avoid claiming formal support, lab relationships, government approval or cultural authority unless those relationships are real and documented.

Good headline

“Shared Table proposal maps a safer path for local food sharing on Straddie.”

Useful quote frame

“The aim is to start small, check everything carefully and learn before scaling.”

Avoid

Do not present the project as launched, funded, endorsed or operational until it is.
