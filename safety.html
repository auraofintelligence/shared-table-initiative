Safety and protocol

Safe first, permission first, public later.

The Shared Table only works if people trust the checks. Foraged, unusual or culturally sensitive materials need a stronger gate than ordinary pantry donations.

Non-negotiables

  • No wild or unusual foods without correct permissions and safety checks.
  • No cultural knowledge is published without custodial approval.
  • No private donor, participant or health details go into public notices.
  • No lab, council or organisation is named as a partner unless that is true.

Safety language

Confidence comes from process, not bravado.

The public copy should avoid inflated phrases like “guaranteed pure” or “world-class safety” unless there is real evidence behind them. A better promise is smaller: clear labels, safe storage, permissioned sourcing and review before public sharing.

Use

“Checked by the table crew before sharing.”

Use with proof

“Lab tested” only when there is a real current test result.

Avoid

Do not imply endorsement, native-title permission or formal partnership without written basis.
