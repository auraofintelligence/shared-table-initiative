Private boards are food-front logistics.

Most resilience work belongs inside trusted groups: garden harvests, pantry gaps, freezer space, transport, meal timing, preserving days, allergies and who quietly needs a hand.

Good private boards

  • Family or neighbour pantry and garden audits.
  • Club kitchen rosters, preserving days and working bee meals.
  • Elders, carers or youth support meal planning.
  • Disaster-ready street check-ins and pantry gaps.
  • Flexible meal times for breakfast, brunch, lunch, afternoon, evening, late and split-shift routines.

When a post goes public, make it smaller.

A public notice should only carry what strangers need: what is happening, when, where, how to contact the organiser, when the notice expires and what safety limits apply. The real food map stays with the trusted group.

Public-safe

General calls for produce, helpers, clean containers, preserving days, working bee meals or open cook-up times.

Keep private

Addresses, health details, vulnerable people, private donor lists, exact household pantry gaps and unverified claims.

Paper fallback

Print simple versions for kitchens, clubs, ferry noticeboards or offline days.
